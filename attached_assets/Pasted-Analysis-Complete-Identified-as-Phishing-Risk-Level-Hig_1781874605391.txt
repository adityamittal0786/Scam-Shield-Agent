The Bottom Line
This message is a trick! It pretends to be from India Post saying your parcel is stuck and asks for a small fee to redeliver it. But the website link is fake, and they just want to steal your money or personal details. Don't click it!

Red Flags Detected
•
The domain 'indiapost-track-update.net' is not the official India Post domain (which is indiapost.gov.in), indicating a fake website.
•
The message is an unsolicited request for a small, unexpected fee for parcel redelivery, a common tactic in phishing scams.
•
It uses urgency ('Failure to complete payment today') to pressure the recipient into immediate action without critical verification.
•
The message is generic, lacking specific parcel details or the recipient's name, which legitimate postal notifications usually include.
What You Should Do
Do not click on the provided link or enter any personal or financial information.
Verify the status of any expected parcels directly through the official India Post website or app, using a tracking number you already possess.
Report the message to India Post and your mobile service provider to help prevent others from falling victim.
Block the sender's number to prevent further scam attempts.

How Scammers Hook You
The step-by-step attack chain this scammer uses to extract money or data.
1
**Impersonation**: Send a message pretending to be from India Post, a trusted and recognized postal service.
2
**Problem Creation**: Fabricate a delivery issue (incomplete address) to create a sense of urgency and concern for the recipient.
3
**Demand for Payment**: Request a small, seemingly reasonable 'redelivery fee' (Rs 99) to make the request appear legitimate and low-risk.
4
**Phishing Link**: Provide a malicious URL designed to mimic an official payment portal, aiming to harvest financial details or personal information.
5
**Pressure Tactic**: Apply time pressure ('Failure to complete payment today') to rush the victim into action without critical thinking.
6
**Financial Gain/Credential Harvesting**: Once the victim clicks the link and enters details, the scammer either steals money directly or collects credentials for future fraudulent activities.
